# Testimonials

## What It Is

A way to collect client testimonials directly from your website and choose which ones appear publicly. A visitor fills out a short form on your site; you review what they wrote, edit it if needed, and approve or reject it. Nothing is ever shown on your site until you approve it.

## Add the Form to Your Website

1. Go to **My Website** > edit a page (or create one, e.g. a page called "Testimonials").
2. Choose **Testimonial Submission Form** beside **Add Block**, then click **Add Block**.
3. Set a heading and any supporting text, then **Save Block**.
4. Publish the page.

The block renders the submission form. Once you've approved one or more testimonials, they appear in a list underneath the form on that same page — nobody sees a submission until you approve it.

Some business types/packages may already include a starter "Testimonials" page with this block pre-added; you can still add the block to any other page yourself.

## What a Visitor Sees and Submits

The public form asks for first name, last name, email, and the testimonial text. It's protected by the same spam defenses used on your contact form (hidden honeypot field, a minimum-fill-time check, and a simple math question) — genuine visitors filling it out normally are never affected.

## Review Submissions

1. Select **Testimonials** in the Agent Portal menu (under Website).
2. New submissions land under the **Pending** tab.
3. Click **Edit** to read the full text and adjust wording (fix typos, tone down anything inappropriate) before approving.
4. Click **Approve** to publish it to your site, or **Reject** to keep it hidden without deleting it.
5. Use the **Approved** / **Rejected** / **All** tabs to see everything you've reviewed. Click the trash icon to permanently delete a submission.

Approving or rejecting doesn't require re-editing first — you can act directly from the Pending list, or open **Edit** first if you want to adjust the wording.

## Package Availability

Testimonials is included with every package. If a package removes access to it later, agents keep any testimonials already approved and visible on their site, but can no longer add new **Testimonial Submission Form** blocks or reach the review queue until they upgrade.
